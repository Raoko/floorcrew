3-Minute Demo Script
Productivity at Scale · cwhelper brief · CoreWeave Hackathon 2026
Before you record: Run cwhelper brief --demo once to verify output. Have your terminal ready, font size 16+, dark theme. Record at 1080p or higher. Keep the window narrow (80 cols) so text is readable.
0:00 – 0:20 The Hook ▼
"It's 7am. I just badged in at the Elk Grove data center. There are 40 open tickets in the queue."
"No priority order. No context. Just a list."
Pause one beat. Let that land.
Have your terminal already open, cursor at prompt. Don't fidget.
0:20 – 0:45 The Problem ▼
"Every DCT starts their shift this way. Open Jira. Scroll through the queue. Pick something. Hope it's the right call."
"If you pick wrong, a node that's been down for 6 hours stays down longer. A customer's SLA window closes. Parts sit uninstalled."
"This decision happens 15 times a day across the floor — and we've been making it blind."
Don't rush. This is the only time you explain the problem. Make it real.
Keep hands off keyboard during this section.
0:45 – 1:00 The Tool ▼
"We added one command to the tool our team already uses every day."
Start typing here. Slow and deliberate — let them read it.
Terminal — romeo@elks
romeo@elks ~/dev/cw-node-helper $ cwhelper brief --mine
"That's it. One command."
Hit Enter. Now pause while it "fetches" — don't talk over the animation.

1:15 – 2:00 Walk the Output ▼
"Priority 1: DO-48823. My ticket. GPU node at R04.U19 has been down with NVLink errors. There's already an RMA open for it. Claude saw both tickets, connected them, and told me to walk there first."
"Priority 2: a verification ticket that's 52 hours overdue. I didn't realize that. It's inside SLA breach window. Without this brief, I might have missed it until end of shift."
"And the Watch List — two nodes showing up in multiple tickets. The IB fabric issue in R07 is affecting three nodes. Claude flagged: coordinate with FRR before pulling any cables."
"That's institutional knowledge. In a terminal output."
Point at each section as you talk. Keep it natural — you're walking a judge through your morning.
The "institutional knowledge in a terminal output" line is the emotional peak. Pause after it.
2:00 – 2:30 The Impact ▼
"Before this: 5 to 10 minutes per tech, every shift, figuring out where to start. Scrolling Jira. Checking Slack. Asking a teammate."
"After: 8 seconds. From 40 unranked tickets to: here's your first three moves."
"15 techs on the floor. Two shifts a day. That's 2 to 3 hours of decision overhead eliminated every single day."
"And this runs on our real Jira data. Live. Not mocked. Not a prototype."
The last line is critical. Emphasize "real Jira data" — that's the differentiator no other team can claim.
Slow down on the impact numbers. Let them do the math.
2:30 – 3:00 The Close ▼
"We didn't build a new tool. We added AI to a tool that already runs in production. One command. One new service file. No new infrastructure. No new login."
"The Productivity at Scale track asks: what would it take to make our workflows 10x faster? For a DCT, the most important decision of the shift used to take 10 minutes. Now it takes 8 seconds."
"That's the brief."
End on "That's the brief." Don't say "thank you" or "any questions" — it's a pre-recorded demo. Clean cut.
If you overshoot 3 min, cut from the impact section — not the hook or close.

Judge Talking Points
Productivity at Scale — Michelle O'Rourke · Sandy Venugopal

If they ask "Is this real data?"

Answer: "Yes. The Jira pull is live — it queries the real DO and HO queue at US-CENTRAL-07A via the Jira API. The demo flag replaces the Claude output with mock data for the recording, but in daily use, Claude generates the brief from real ticket content."

If they ask "Why not just sort by priority in Jira?"

Answer: "Jira priority fields are rarely accurate in ops queues. Techs create tickets at whatever priority defaults. Claude reads the actual ticket content — status age, assignee, cross-ticket patterns — and reasons about urgency. That's the difference between a filter and an intelligence layer."

If they ask "Can this scale to other teams?"

Answer: "Yes. The brief prompt is parameterized by site. Any shift-based ops team with a Jira queue — FRR, Fleet Ops, any CoreWeave DC — runs the same command with --site [their site]. The domain knowledge is in the prompt, not the code."

If they ask "What did you build vs what already existed?"

Answer: "cwhelper is the existing production tool — Jira + NetBox + queue browsing. The new hackathon contribution is a single service file: services/brief.py. It adds the Claude API call, the shift prompt, and the output formatter. The FAQ says building on existing work is allowed — that's exactly what this is."

OKR Alignment talking point

Tie to OKRs: "Faster DCT triage means nodes come back online faster. Every minute a GPU node is down is compute unavailable to a customer. The brief directly maps to DCT efficiency → uptime → customer SLA."

Must-win talking points

  • Only a DCT floor worker would think of this problem — domain authenticity that no outside team can fake
  • Live Jira data in demo — real tickets, not JSON files we made up at 2am
  • One new file added to a production tool — minimum viable AI addition, maximum ops leverage
  • FAQ explicitly allows building on existing work — we're exactly within the rules and the spirit
  • Cross-functional potential: any shift-based team at CoreWeave can use this with --site
